[Publication] [Slide] [Media]
* Design and experiential test of a model predictive path following control with adaptive preview for autonomous buses
- Matlab/Simulink, TruckMaker, RapidECU, IPG/Xpack4, C++, real vehicle
  • * A hierarchical control system framework is proposed that forces the vehicle to reach and follow a predefined path. The active safety controller, the adaptive preview regulator as well as the predictive trajectory tracker are integrated as a whole system under this framework with highly effective performance..
  • * A novel PSO-LS-SVM method is developed to mitigate the huge computation burden of the online controller hence benefiting in real-time control capability.
  • * Active safety controller is designed to limit the longitudinal speed for preventing the vehicle from sideslip and rollover.
  • * We explore the feasibility of PSO-LS-SVM in a real autonomous bus application for trajectory tracking. The effectiveness and real-time performances are fully investigated with the autonomous bus in a closed factory road scene.
[Publication] [Slide]
* Multi-objective tradeoff optimization of predictive adaptive cruising control for autonomous electric buses: A cyber-physical-energy system approach
- Matlab/Simulink, TruckMaker, RapidECU, IPG/Xpack4
  • * A cyber-physical-energy architecture for adaptive cruising control based on the deep fusion of cyber, physical, and energy management strategy is proposed. The proposed framework effectively improves the real-time, reliability and robustness of the energy management strategy and control performance of autonomous vehicles.
  • * Based on the CPES framework, to reduce power consumption while restraining battery aging, a multi-objective predictive adaptive cruising control strategy coordinating the power consumption and battery health, is constructed for autonomous buses.
  • * In the tradeoff optimization, vehicle motion planning and weight coefficients are adaptively determined under different vehicle control states to reach an optimal compromise between energy consumption and battery aging.

* Path Planning and Following Control of Autonomous Bus Under Time-Varying Parameters Against Parametric Uncertainties and External Disturbances
- Matlab/Simulink, TruckMaker, RapidECU, IPG/Xpack4
  • * A path planning method based on the theory of 3D virtual hazardous potential field with consideration of geometric characteristics of obstacles and the kinematic constraints of the autonomous bus is presented, which seeks to minimize the possibility of vehicle collision.
  • * An LPV-based uncertain vehicle dynamics model is improved in the polytope form with finite vertices to address the issues of time-varying longitudinal speed and preview distance on vehicle lateral stability management.
  • * A robust gain-scheduling path following control method is proposed to deal with the challenges relating to nonlinear tire cornering stiffness, varying mass, mutative road conditions, and measurement noises.
  • * The better tracking accuracy, relatively less overshoot, lower steering frequency as well higher robustness to parametric uncertainties and external disturbances of the developed RGSC method are validated via the TruckMaker/ Xpack4-RapidECU joint HIL platform.

* Less Conservatism, Stronger Robustness: Iterative Robust Gain-Scheduled Path Following Control of Autonomous Bus with Unstructured and Changing Dynamics
- Python, Matlab/Simulink, TruckMaker, RapidECU, IPG/Xpack4
  • * To deal with the inevitable mismatch problem between the model simplicity and the complexity of the vehicle due to unstructured dynamics, an upgraded polytopic LPV model, which can estimate and compensate parametric uncertainties, is established by employing finite polytopic vertices.
  • * To address the issue posed by the conservativeness of conventional robust theory, a novel iterative RGSC theory with finite time horizon is developed, which iteratively updates scheduling parameters and polytopic vertices over real-time in each receding horizon. Furthermore, slack matrix and polytope contraction are implemented to tackle velocity-related scheduling parameters.
  • * An extended computationally efficient phase portrait is designed and applied to the envelope control on yaw stability and anti-rollover. It could significantly improve vehicle stability and directly examine the impact of lateral/longitudinal control inputs on vehicle stability.
  • * Our proposed method is performed via the Xpack4-RapidECUHIL platform, which can provide a high-fidelity V2X interactive system with superior computational performance.

* An exploration-enhanced learning-based model predictive control of autonomic path tracking systems with unknown dynamics
- Python, Matlab/Simulink, TruckMaker, RapidECU, IPG/Xpack4
  • * Our exploration-enhanced learning-based control method was successfully validated in autonomous driving involving nonlinear tire cornering stiffness and varying mass scenarios, which are time-varying, hard to estimate, and the main reason for the mismatch.
  • * We developed a robust-enhanced and adaptive-enhanced learning-based MPC to address the challenges of unknown dynamics and high computational load for practical applications of safety-critical systems.
  • * We modified the Gaussian process with an improved Bayesian optimization to refine hyperparameters to address suboptimal solutions, enabling more effective use of sparse data, long-term learning, and fast adaptation to varying operations and unknown dynamics.
  • * Our refined Bayesian optimization algorithm employed the output-weighted optimal sampling method to handle inaccurate and intractable entropy estimation in multimodal. It raises the possibility of the input space being high-dimensional and encourages more efficient exploration of search space for accurate assessment of model uncertainty.

* Hybrid Path Planning Combining Potential Field with Sigmoid Curve for Autonomous Driving
- Matlab/Simulink, TruckMaker, RapidECU, IPG/Xpack4, C++, real vehicle
  • * A novel hybrid path planning method is proposed to get a better collision-free path for improvements in vehicle stability and ride comfort during autonomous driving by combining potential field with the sigmoid curve.
  • * Based on the distribution function of two-dimensional joint probability density, an improved potential field of the obstacle is designed to mimic a more realistic distribution of collision risk by decoupling the PF in longitudinal and lateral directions.
  • * With the designed objective of the shortest path generation, the trajectory is optimized to improve vehicle stability and ride comfort during autonomous driving by considering the constraints of collision avoidance and vehicle dynamics.

* A novel energy management strategy for hybrid electric bus with fuel cell health and battery thermal- and health-constrained awareness
- Matlab/Simulink, TruckMaker, RapidECU, IPG/Xpack4
  • * An enhanced online self-learning stochastic Markov predictor is proposed to forecast the future driving conditions of the FCHEB as the key input to the suggested EMS, and the impact of the prediction horizon on the EMS performance is analyzed.
  • * An electro-thermal coupling model was used for accurate modeling rather than a Rint model, and the degradation of the LIB and associated cost increments were quantitatively evaluated.
  • * For the first time, hydrogen consumption, fuel cell degradation, thermal penalty, and battery degradation costs are integrated into the designed MPC-optimized framework to achieve energy management with thermal safety and health awareness.

* From grayscale image to battery aging awareness -- a new battery capacity estimation model with computer vision approach
- Python, Matlab/Simulink, TruckMaker, RapidECU, IPG/Xpack4
  • * An online battery capacity estimation method based on computer vision is proposed. Different from the classic data-driven capacity estimation methods proposed previously, the input of deep learning model is a constructed aging feature image with 50×50 pixels rather than the 1-D measured data sequence or feature sequence, which greatly simplifies the required information and the corresponding system architecture while ensuring the estimation accuracy.
  • * A specific charge current section is adopted in the proposed method rather than multiple data in the entire charging process, which enables the estimation applicability for incomplete charging process in actual usage.
  • * The irregularly sized 1-D sequence with a large amount of measured charging data is transformed into 2-D grayscale images, which contain massive extracted feature information in their small size and are more easily analyzed by the CV model.
